Jewels By the Sea

✦ A website redesign to bring an eclectic jewelry store into the modern age


Timeline

8 weeks (2023)

Role

1 of 4 UX designers +
Client liaison

Tools

Figma ○ FigJam ○ Discord ○ Google Suite

Summary

Jewels By The Sea is closing its physical location after 20+ years. The owner plans to shift to online sales and occasional pop-up booths. However, their website hasn't been updated in 7 years.

Our team set out to modernize online presence while maintaining the store's personality and fulfilling user and owner needs. We delivered a Figma prototype ready for handoff and I was contracted to develop the site this summer!

Understanding our Client


After securing Jewels By the Sea as our client, our first step was to conduct a client brief to gain a deeper understanding of the owner and the business. Our team visited the store in person and prepared a set of questions.

Understanding our Users

We decided on splitting users into two major categories: regular customers and potential customers. We conducted 10 interviews. 4 were phone interviews with regular customers, while the remaining 6 were in-person ad hoc style interviews near the Jewels By The Sea store.




*Research Limitations: Convenience sampling, Selection bias


Regular Customers

  • Motivation for repeated visits➞ The owner's expertise in gems, personalized shopping experience, and ability to match pieces with customers

  • Believe the current website is outdated and fails to adequately showcase the quality jewelry pieces and owner's warm and trustworthy personality.

  • Regularly shop for unique holiday and birthday gifts at Jewels By the Sea

  • Staff expertise and quality of service are most important when shopping for jewelry

Potential Customers

  • Tend to purchase jewelry infrequently, typically 0-1 times per month

  • Appreciate the flexibility to shop both online and in-person ➞ enjoy being able to try on pieces and receive advice, however time constraints may sometimes limit in-person visits

  • Sometimes struggle knowing what to look for if they don't regularly shop for jewelry

  • Quality of service and price are most important when shopping for jewelry


For both groups ➞ Earrings, rings and necklaces were the most frequently purchased items!

Design Objectives

  1. Maintain branding by keeping a similar color scheme & logos to avoid confusion for current customers

  2. Feature an “Events” page where users can find where owner will be setting up pop-up booths

  3. Distinguish “One of a kind” jewelry from “recurring” items

  4. Promote signing up to receive the newsletter so that customers can stay updated

  5. Improve browsing for jewelry as well as letting users order online

  6. Highlight owner's credibility through showcasing her credentials, awards, and testimonials

Ideating

Competitive Analysis

We analyzed brand, features & functionality, navigation, site architecture, and content of different e-commerce jewelry websites that were either located near Jewels By the Sea or were well-established.

This helped us better understand the key components for an effective e-commerce jewelry website.


The main websites we analyzed were: Bvlgari, Swarovski, Gorjana, David & Sons Fine Jewelry, and Kranser Jewelers. We also analyzed some aspects of: Bluenile and Mejuri.


Brand

  • Simple, clean logos and large, detailed images are the prevailing methods for visually reflecting a brand's identity and mood

Features & Functionality

  • For optimal user experience ➞ Simplify categorization, prioritize contact information, offer flexible item filtering, and have short newsletter subscription forms

Navigation

  • Ensure consistent navigation across viewports and incorporate clickable images for less text-heavy navigation bars.

Site Architecture

  • Save users' time by making contact information clickable links, while ensuring intuitive and user-friendly organization of the navigation bar

Content

  • Information about the jewelry + history and credentials about the brand should be present

Wireframing

With our design objectives defined and information gathered from our competitive analysis we began wireframing possible designs for the redesign.

Prototyping & Testing

After wireframing, our team created Lo-fi and Hi-fi prototypes for both mobile and desktop platforms. We conducted usability testing after the completion of each prototype and iterated our designs based on user insights. For our usability testing, we first had participants complete various tasks/scenarios and then asked them a few questions afterwards.


Lo-fi Prototype



    ✔ "Pairs Well With" section in product detail pages

    ✔ User freedom in accessing product pages

    ✗ Confusion regarding the purpose of the "Events" page due to unclear wording in question asked

    • Question was improved before next testing round

    Overall thoughts ➞ Intuitive prototypes & information was displayed nicely


Want to know more?


Hi-fi Prototype



    ✔ "Add to Cart" button was easily accessible

    ✔ "Pairs Well With" section was appreciated

    ✔ Shopping and checkout process was found to be intuitive

    ✔ Events page effectively conveyed information

    ━ "Jewelry Appraisals" section on the homepage initially confused users, but the service description clarified its purpose

    • We considered renaming the section to "Services," but ultimately kept the name as "Jewelry Appraisals" and added an image of an appraisal to highlight the specific service offered and improve user understanding

    ✗ "More about us" button in the hero section of the homepage went unnoticed by users, leading to difficulty in finding it when asked

    • After exploring various placements and styles, it was deemed redundant and removed as users primarily used the navigation bar to access the about page

Hi-fi Prototype Feedback

For our Hi-fi Prototype we also received feedback from another group in our course, teaching staff, and our client. We used this feedback along with our usability testing insights to iterate once more and finalize all components of our design.

✹ Takeaways

Client Communication = Project Success

  • This was my first experience designing for a business owner. Now, looking back, I attribute proactive, transparent client communication and having a deep understanding of their needs as the foundations for our success.

Embrace Iteration

  • During this project, I practiced not becoming overly attached to early designs. I realized the significance of embracing iteration as a continuous process to enhance and refine user experience. In other words, I just kept on iterating!

It's Always Time to Learn!

  • Throughout this project, I learned new design techniques and approaches from working alongside my teammates and also through independent exploration. I believe this experience significantly expanded my skill set and contributed to my personal growth.

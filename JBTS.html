Jewels By the Sea

E-commerce website redesign | 8 weeks | 2023

Overview

Jewels By The Sea is a unique jewelry shop in La Jolla, California.

The owner is retiring and closing the physical store location, but still plans to sell jewelry pieces at nearby farmer's markets and online.

Problem

The Jewel's By the Sea website is outdated, it has not been updated in 7 years.



Project Goal ➞ Modernizing online presence to compensate for physical store closure, while maintaining the shop's personality and fulfilling owner's requirements

Outcomes

✓ Finalized Figma prototype ready for handoff

✓ New Figma skills

✓ Summer job to develop the site!

Understanding our Users

Interview Insights


Regular Customers

  1. Motivation for repeated visits➞ The owner's expertise in gems, personalized shopping experience, and ability to match pieces with customers

  2. Believe the current website is outdated and fails to adequately showcase the quality jewelry pieces and owner's personality.

  3. Regularly shop for unique holiday and birthday gifts at Jewels By the Sea

  4. Staff expertise and quality of service are most important when shopping for jewelry

Potential Customers

  1. Tend to purchase jewelry infrequently, typically 0-1 times per month

  2. Appreciate the flexibility to shop both online and in-person ➞ enjoy being able to try on pieces and receive advice, however time constraints may sometimes limit in-person visits

  3. Sometimes struggle knowing what to look for if they don't regularly shop for jewelry

  4. Quality of service and price are most important when shopping for jewelry


For both groups ➞ Earrings, rings and necklaces were the most frequently purchased!




Our team conducted 10 interviews. 4 were phone interviews with regular customers, while the remaining 6 were in-person ad hoc style interviews near Jewels By The Sea. To gather valuable insights, we prepared two versions of interview questions, one for regular customers and one for potential customers.

*Research Limitations: Convenient Sampling, Selection Bias ─ Data may not be fully representative

Design Objectives

  1. Maintain branding by keeping a similar color scheme & logos to avoid confusion for current customers

  2. Feature an “Events” page where users can find where owner will be setting up pop-up booths

  3. Distinguish “One of a kind” jewelry from “recurring” items

  4. Promote signing up to receive the newsletter so that customers can stay updated

  5. Improve browsing for jewelry as well as letting users order online

  6. Highlight owner's credibility through showcasing her credentials, awards, and testimonials
